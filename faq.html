Parent FAQ

Questions families ask before enrolling.

I2I Youth Lab is designed for students who want to learn STEM through real community problem-solving.

What is I2I Youth Lab?

A youth STEM internship where students learn data analytics and apply it to issues like food insecurity and homelessness.

What will my child actually do?

Students analyze real datasets, learn basic programming, create maps and visualizations, work in teams, and present solutions.

Does my child need coding experience?

No. The program is beginner-friendly and introduces programming step-by-step.

What age group is this for?

Middle school to high school students, with content that can be adjusted by cohort level.

How long is the program?

Typically 3–6 weeks during the summer.

What is the cost?

The program cost is $550 per student. Scholarships are available.

Is this like a regular camp?

No. It is structured like an internship experience focused on technical learning, teamwork, and impact.

Who teaches the program?

Instructors with experience in data analytics, research, and community development.
